THE TOMATOMETER
Now that you’re a tomatometer expert, go see whats fresh and start watching!
The tomatometer shows how many positive reviews a movie or television show recieves from our trusted critics.
See a score of 75? That means 75 percent of reviews for that movie or show were positive. Anything that recieves a rating of 60 or higher is FRESH. Only scored 45? Consider it ROTTEN.
A movie needs at least 5 reviews to be scored, so if you don’t see a score it might not have enough reviews. Or, it just might not be out yet.
When a movie consistently scores a 75 or better and has been reviewed by at least 5 of our top critics we call that CERTIFIED FRESH. When you see a movie that’s certified, consider it required viewing.
Now, what if you want to know what viewers like you think? Sure, the critics love it, but does that mean I will too?
In addition to our critics, we also offer an audience score. If at least 60% percent of users give a positive rating, you better believe thats FRESH. On the flip side, if its mostly negative ratings, we’re going to have to call that ROTTEN. If there is no audience score, chances are the movie or show doesn’t have anough reviews or it’s just not out yet.
